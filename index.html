Art 292 Multimedia I/Web Design

Course Description

Art292 Multimedia I, focuses on the fundamentals of web-based art and design as they are crucial for the contemporary multimedia society. We will explore web-based art and design for aesthetic and critical purposes with hand-making, digital, social, and ciritical thinking skills. Specifically, we will explore technologies of HTML, CSS, p5.js, prototyping, and AI to integrate various media forms: text, image, audio, video, etc. Throughout the course, discussions on the practice, criticism, and theory of web-based art and design will also be held to hone the studentâ€™s understanding of the subject. (Pre-requisite: ART109).

Course Objectives

  • Our ability to see not just how humans use multimedia technologies but how the multimedia design constructs us as humans
  • Our technical knowledge of and ability to manipulate multimedia materials and tools
  • Our potential to utilize code and multimedia as intentional components within art and design practices
  • Our capacity to assess the power relations around multimedia
  • Our understanding of how multimedia has been used in art and design and beyond in order to place our works within a larger historical context

Attendance Policy

Physical attendance in the classroom is required. Attendance is taken at each class. If at some point in the semester you cannot physically attend class sessions due to illness, injury, or other approved absence, you must contact the instructor immediately, to make arrangements for how to best proceed with instruction and course work.
